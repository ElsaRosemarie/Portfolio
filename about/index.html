Hi! I am Elsa (they/she), a queer illustrator, graphic designer and anthropologist living in Utrecht, the Netherlands. Ever since I could hold a pencil, being creative is how I have made a place for myself in the world. Driven by an ever-present need to create, I decided to study industrial design in 2018. Here, I was quickly drawn into the world of social design, inspired by the radical voices of the climate and social justice movement I had become engaged in. For my master's, I switched to anthropology, where I did my thesis on the relearning of more-than-human entanglement and the use of arts-based research methods. I feel most fulfilled in my work, when I can combine digging into complex literature with facilitating workshops and translating insights into art, which is why I have always done illustration and graphic design work next to my studies. Whether I was filling sketchbooks with visions of a better world, designing the magazine of my study association, or creating the visual identity for a climate movement, my hands are always itching to explore and create meaning.

In 2026, I decided to fully focus on my illustration and graphic design career, often letting myself be inspired by my research background. Fascinated by the complexity of relationships – both human as well as more-than-human, my colourful work seeks openings to intimacy that embrace softer ways of being, while acknowledging the contradictions, imperfections, and struggles of entanglement. In my commissioned work, I have specialised in creating publications and visual identities, often for academic and activist clients. I feel deeply fulfilled by creating visuals to someone else's words and sharing printed copies with the world. In the future, I hope to keep developing my skills and share them with a wider audience of critically engaged dreamers.

When I'm not working, I drink an insane amount of herbal tea, read books about messy gays, cook experimental vegan dinners, spend quality time with my friends, and wander around town with my camera in hand, finding beauty in things that would otherwise go unnoticed.
